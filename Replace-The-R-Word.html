Why Replace the R-Word?

The R-word is the word 'retard(ed)'. Why does it hurt? The R-word hurts because it is exclusive. It's offensive. It's derogatory. Replace the R-Word asks people to discover new ways to express themselves as a way to be more mindful of how your words can hurt those with disabilities without you even knowing it. The change starts with you, and you are well on your way! Look up new words today to better describe the way you are feeling using respectful, people-first language!

Words can hurt
(but they don't have to).

Think of times when you may use/have used the R-word.

---Did someone at work not perform to your standards?
---Were you reading instructions you thought seemed complicated?
---Did someone paint their house a color you thought was weird?

 The reasons people interchange the R-word for another descriptive word are endless - BUT there are THOUSANDS of descriptive words you can learn to be able to break your habits and stop using the R-word.
Think of how much more intellectual you will sounds with your new vocab!
And most importantly, think of all the people with disabilities who will appreciate your inclusivity!

Spread the Word to End the Word

The Spread the Word to End the Word campaign is supported by Special Olympics, Best Buddies and over 200 other organizations from around the world.
The campaign asks people to pledge to stop saying the R-word as a starting point toward creating more accepting attitudes and communities for all people.
